Overview

The Oscillating Resonant Group AxioN (ORGAN) Collaboration is an Australian-led effort developing resonant detectors to search for axion dark matter and related new physics. ORGAN builds on Australia’s strengths in precision microwave engineering, low-noise electronics, and cryogenic instrumentation, combining expertise from multiple institutions to explore the axion parameter space across a wide range of masses.

The collaboration operates a family of complementary experiments:

  • ORGAN Main – A high-frequency haloscope probing axion masses in the 50–200 μeV range, hosted at the University of Western Australia. It has delivered some of the most sensitive results in this region to date.
  • ORGAN Q – A quantum-enhanced haloscope operating at millikelvin temperatures and employing Josephson Parametric Amplifiers to approach the standard quantum limit and beyond.
  • ORGAN Low – A re-entrant cavity experiment targeting the sub-GHz regime using large, tunable lumped-element resonators, designed to probe cosmologically motivated low-mass axions.

Through these parallel efforts, ORGAN aims to bridge the gap between traditional microwave haloscopes and next-generation quantum-limited detectors, pushing sensitivity both upward and downward in axion mass and opening new territory in the global dark matter search.
